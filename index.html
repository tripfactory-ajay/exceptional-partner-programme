1 · Entry & Recruitment
Eligibility Criteria
Partial
Field team identifies prospects on gut feel + volume + network. No minimum experience criteria.
Defined
Min. 3 years' travel sales experience required. Must have customer-facing holiday selling history, destination knowledge, and drive to run own business.
Risk of onboarding weak profiles that consume support without returns. No standard benchmark.
Define PSA eligibility: min. years in travel, min. annual pax/revenue, existing client base size. Codify in Google Form screening.
Application Funnel
Strong
4-step funnel: Field meeting → Google Form → Leadership screening → Final interview (Sanjay + Ganesh Sir). 26 met, 12 final round, 6 confirmed.
Strong
Discovery call → application → contract → induction. BDM assigned immediately post-signing.
TF relies on leadership judgement rather than a scored rubric. Inconsistent across field team members.
Create a scored interview scorecard aligned to TRSG pillars. Each criterion rated 1–5. Minimum pass score to proceed.
2 · Commercial Structure
Monthly Fee / Cost
Not Defined
No monthly fee structure. EPP appears free. No published cost to PSA for platform, support, or training.
Clear
Zero start-up cost. Ongoing £49+VAT/month. Additional fees if extra staff added. Card processing fees deducted from commission.
Without a fee or commitment mechanism, TF can't recover investment if PSA underperforms or exits early.
Consider a nominal monthly commitment (₹2,000–5,000) or minimum booking target with a structured exit/clawback clause within 12 months.
Commission Structure
Partial
Commission referenced but no % rates or payment timings published. Growth tiers mentioned but not formalised.
Clear
65% of gross profit to PSA. Statement on 15th of each month. Paid last business day of that month. Cancelled bookings: immediate clawback. Commission can scale to 80% (Directors Club).
PSAs have no certainty about what they earn or when. Critical barrier to commitment.
Publish commission schedule by booking type (FIT/GIT/MICE/FD). Define payment timing. Include cancellation clawback. Create tiered commission growth (e.g. 60% → 70% → 80% by volume).
Minimum Sales Target
Not Defined
Growth tiers exist as aspiration but no enforceable minimum. Low performers remain indefinitely.
Enforced
£150,000 gross sales per 12 months. Failure = contractual termination trigger. Reviewed annually.
Inactive PSAs drain TF support resources with no mechanism to exit or upgrade them.
Set tiered targets: Tier 1 PSA (₹50L/year), Tier 2 (₹1.5Cr/year), Tier 3 EPP Elite (₹3Cr+). Review at 6 & 12 months. Tie to partnership benefits and commission tier.
Discounting & Margin
Missing
No discounting or margin guidance for PSAs. No guidance on gross vs net pricing or markup rules.
Documented
PSA controls their margin freely on dynamic packages. Must coordinate with TO for 3rd party package markups. Payment fees must always be covered. Strategic guidance on when not to discount.
PSAs competing with OTAs will discount without understanding the consequences to income and TF minimum targets.
Produce a Discounting & Margin Guide: gross vs net pricing, how to handle price match requests, when NOT to discount, minimum margin to always cover payment costs.
3 · Onboarding & Legal
Formal Agreement
Mentioned
Agreement referenced in Phase 2 SOP but no standard template or signed document in the EPP materials.
Comprehensive
Full 21-clause legal agreement: appointment, commission, IP, data protection, training SLAs, termination, non-compete (1yr post-exit), indemnity. Signed via DocuSign.
Without a signed agreement, TF has no legal recourse if PSA leaves, takes clients, or breaches brand standards.
Draft a standard EPP Partner Agreement: commission terms, minimum targets, non-solicitation, brand usage, termination notice, data handling, exit obligations.
Service Level Commitments
Missing
No formal SLAs for TF or PSA. Support described generically but not measured or enforced.
Contractual
12 LMS modules/year (100%), 8 supplier/destination trainings/year (100%), business plan within 3 months (100%). Service Level Failure = termination trigger.
Without mutual SLAs there is no accountability on either side.
Define mutual SLAs: TF commits to quote TAT within 4hrs, support 6 days/week, monthly review. PSA commits to training modules, business plan, monthly reporting.
4 · Training
Induction Training
Basic
Product, process, sales pitch, system usage mentioned in Phase 2. No structured curriculum, timeline, or completion criteria.
Structured
2-day virtual induction + 1-day business planning with BDM. Brand, pipeline creation, customer finding, tools, systems. LMS access from Day 1.
TF induction is informal. PSAs go live inconsistently prepared. Risk of poor early bookings damaging relationships.
Build a 2–3 day EPP Induction: Day 1 (TF platform + product), Day 2 (booking flow + quotation), Day 3 (market mining + business plan). Virtual via Zoom. Issue completion certificate.
Ongoing Training / LMS
Informal
Destination and supplier training referenced but no LMS, training calendar, or completion tracking exists.
Formalised
Full LMS with ongoing modules. Expert masterclasses on destinations, marketing, business. Familiarisation trip opportunities. Min. 8 supplier/destination trainings/year contractually required.
No self-serve learning resource. Knowledge gaps compound. No tracking to identify under-trained PSAs.
Build a basic LMS (Google Classroom or Notion initially). Quarterly training calendar: destination months, supplier webinars, business skills. Track completions. Add fam trip opportunities as reward.
Business Plan
Missing
Business growth discussed at interview but no formal plan required post-onboarding. No accountability to written targets.
Mandatory
Business plan submitted within 3 months. Dedicated 1-day planning session with BDM. Reviewed quarterly.
PSAs who don't commit to paper are far less likely to execute. Most powerful accountability tool being left unused.
Mandate a 12-month business plan from every EPP partner within 60 days. Provide a standard TF template. Review quarterly with EPP Head. Make this a condition of the first commission payment.
5 · Support Structure
Dedicated Support Person
Shared
Field team collectively manages PSA relationships. Ganesh Sir oversees. No named account manager per PSA.
Named BDM
Every PSA gets a named Business Development Manager. Day-to-day availability. Quarterly formal business reviews. Coach, escalation point, and community builder.
Without a named contact, PSAs feel unsupported. Queries get lost. Field team bandwidth spreads too thin as numbers grow.
Assign each EPP partner a named TF Account Manager. Define role: weekly check-in, monthly performance review, escalation for booking issues. Start with existing field team.
After-Sales / Ops Support
Strong
Phase 4 covers: bookings, amendments, vouchers, itineraries, issue resolution. 24/7 support referenced. Core TF differentiator.
Strong
Head Office admin handles all after-sales centrally. PSA focuses entirely on selling. Amendments, cancellations, supplier payments managed by HO. In-house finance, admin, marketing, IT support.
Both strong. TF needs to market this more explicitly as a core recruitment benefit.
Create a one-page "What TF Does For You" document for PSA recruitment: "We handle all amendments, vouchers, supplier payments and on-trip issues so you can focus 100% on selling."
Complaints Process
Partial
Issue resolution mentioned in Phase 4 but no documented complaints SOP for PSAs. No escalation path, timeline, or form to complete.
Documented
Three-tier process: (1) In-resort with supplier. (2) Written complaint within 28 days of return — PSA facilitates. (3) Escalation to HO via Teams Portal. BDM advises. Mid-trip check-in recommended.
Without a complaints SOP, PSAs either ignore complaints (legal risk) or make promises TF can't keep (financial risk).
Publish a PSA Complaints Handling Guide. Define in-resort path → post-return 28-day window → TF HO escalation → resolution timeline. Encourage mid-trip WhatsApp check-in with all clients.
6 · Marketing & Growth
Brand & Marketing Materials
Partial
Brochures, pitch decks, campaign templates, WhatsApp/email formats mentioned. Rotary/BNI market mining grid is genuinely MORE advanced than Travelosophers.
Personalised
Personalised business name, unique logo, email signature, dedicated microsite page, optional personal phone number, monthly social media assets, co-branded POS materials, personal quotation docs.
TF's market mining strategy is world-class but PSAs lack personalised marketing identity. No city-specific co-branded toolkit per PSA.
Create co-branded EPP marketing kits per PSA: branded WhatsApp templates (city-specific), personalised pitch deck with PSA name and market focus, monthly social media asset pack. Low cost, high impact.
Conferences & Events
Missing
No PSA conference, community events, or networking events referenced in the EPP programme.
Annual Programme
Annual overseas conference, annual UK conference, regional dinner events, supplier events. Community of Travelosophers encouraged as peer support network.
No community = no peer accountability, no knowledge sharing, no shared identity. PSAs feel isolated and are less loyal to the programme.
Launch an annual EPP conference (even a 1-day event in Bangalore or Mumbai). Add quarterly regional meetups per zone. Create a dedicated EPP WhatsApp community for peer support and best practice sharing.
Supplier Incentives Tracker
Not Shared
TF has strong supplier relationships but no incentives tracker or tactical commission overrides are shared with PSAs.
Maintained
Live incentives tracker: Gold Medal Farebanks, TTC £40/booking, G Adventures 25% peaks, EasyJet £400 off, Riviera 10% off. TTO commission list (15%) by destination tracked and updated monthly.
PSAs miss extra earning opportunities. Makes the programme feel unrewarding vs competitors who communicate deals regularly.
Create a live EPP Incentives Tracker (shared Google Sheet). Share monthly supplier tactical deals with all EPP partners. Highlight destination-specific commission uplifts. Update at start of each month.
Payment & Fraud Guidance
Missing
No guidance on payment handling, fraud prevention, or third-party payment risks.
Documented
Clear 3rd party payment policy: no card payments from non-travellers. Bank transfer acceptable. Explicit fraud risk warning. PSA personally liable if policy breached without HO approval.
India's travel market has significant fraud risk. PSAs transacting without guidance expose TF to financial and reputational damage.
Add a Payment & Fraud Awareness module to EPP onboarding. Cover: approved payment methods, red flags for fraudulent bookings, third-party payment risks, when to escalate immediately to TF ops team.
7 · Legal & Compliance
Regulatory Framework
Implicit
TF operates within India's travel framework. No compliance guide or obligations checklist provided to PSAs.
Full
ABTA (P8318) + ATOL + IATA + Advantage Travel Partnership (£4.5bn collective). Full legal framework: PTRs, UK GDPR, Bribery Act 2010, Modern Slavery Act. Dynamic Packaging ATOL process fully trained. Professional Indemnity insurance.
TF PSAs don't know their regulatory responsibilities under India's consumer protection law, GST on travel, or what TF's licence does and doesn't cover.
Create an India Regulatory Compliance Brief for EPP partners: consumer protection obligations, GST on travel services, data handling basics, what TF's IATA/licence covers vs PSA's own responsibility. Plain language, 1–2 pages.

Travelosophers Business Models → TripFactory EPP Tier Recommendations

Travelosophers offers 5 distinct entry routes. TripFactory currently has one. Here's how TF could adopt a tiered model.

Travelosophers — 5 Business Options (UK)

Rather than one-size-fits-all, Travelosophers segments its partners by experience level and business type. This lets them recruit much more widely while giving the right support level to each group. TripFactory's EPP currently treats all PSAs the same regardless of whether they're a 15-year veteran or a 1-year-old agency.

ELITE PACKAGE

Travel Sales Professional

Experienced homeworkers / retail agents
Who: Min. 3 years' travel sales experience, customer-facing, destination expertise.

  • 65% commission from day one
  • 2-day virtual induction
  • Dedicated BDM
  • Full platform access
  • Personalised brand & microsite
  • Dynamic packaging capabilities
Start-up: FREE · Monthly: £49+VAT · Sales Target: £150,000/yr
STANDARD PACKAGE

Travel Industry Professional

1–3 yrs travel sales OR 4+ yrs in travel industry
Who: Less pure sales experience but solid industry background (ops, product, airline, hotel etc).

  • 60% commission starting point
  • Extended induction support
  • More structured training requirement
  • BDM with closer monitoring
  • Pathway to Elite package
Start-up: FREE · Monthly: from £49+VAT
RETURN PACKAGE

Returning to Travel

Out of travel for 5+ years
Who: Previously in travel but career break of 5+ years. Need refresher on systems, products and selling.

  • Extended induction programme
  • Refresher destination training
  • Lower initial targets while ramping up
  • Pathway to Standard → Elite
Start-up: FREE · Monthly: from £49+VAT
AGENCY PACKAGE

Travel Agency Owner

Existing or new travel agency
Who: Agency owners who want to plug into Travelosophers' infrastructure, licensing, and supplier terms.

  • Bespoke commercial agreement
  • Higher volume targets
  • Multi-staff training
  • Wholesale supplier access
  • Agency-level support
Start-up: Contact · Monthly: from £99+VAT
OWN BRAND

Own Brand Partner

Use your own brand within the network
Who: Established agents with their own brand who want TF's infrastructure but not the Travelosophers name.

  • Use own brand throughout
  • Access to all back-end systems
  • Higher monthly fee (premium)
  • Full ABTA/ATOL protection
Start-up: Contact · Pricing: dependent on experience

Recommended TripFactory EPP — 4-Tier Model

Based on TripFactory's current pipeline (26 agents across South, West, Central, North, East India), here is a proposed tiered model that mirrors the Travelosophers approach but is adapted for the Indian travel market context.

Key insight: Looking at the 26 agents already in TF's pipeline, they span a huge range — from 15-year veterans like Journey Maker (Bhopal, ₹12Cr turnover, 700 pax) to 1-year-old agencies like CNearMe Holidays (Bangalore, ₹1.5Cr, 2 staff). Treating them identically in terms of support, targets, and commission makes no sense. A tiered model solves this immediately.
EPP ELITE

Tier 3 — Senior Partner

Equivalent to Travelosophers Travel Sales Professional
Who: 5+ years in travel, 500+ pax/yr, ₹5Cr+ turnover, established client base, multiple staff, ready to scale aggressively.

From current pipeline: Journey Maker, RC Holiday, Subhash Travelastic, Zero Mile Holidays, Multiple Task Holiday, Vasunadhara

  • Highest commission tier (e.g. 70–75%)
  • Named TF Account Director
  • Co-funded marketing budget
  • Priority booking support SLA
  • Invitation to annual TF EPP conference
  • Access to exclusive product deals
Target: ₹3Cr+ gross/year · Monthly commitment: ₹5,000
EPP GROWTH

Tier 2 — Growth Partner

Equivalent to Travelosophers Travel Industry Professional
Who: 2–5 years in travel, 200–500 pax/yr, ₹1.5–5Cr turnover, growing team, clear ambition to scale.

From current pipeline: Fabhnet, Goimomi Holidays, Tailored Experiences, Travel Again Holiday, Vayudoot, Jakariya Holidays

  • Mid commission tier (e.g. 65%)
  • Named TF Account Manager
  • Structured quarterly business reviews
  • Access to full training programme
  • Co-branded marketing materials
  • Path to Tier 3 at ₹3Cr milestone
Target: ₹1.5Cr gross/year · Monthly commitment: ₹3,000
EPP STARTER

Tier 1 — Starter Partner

Equivalent to Travelosophers Return to Travel
Who: 1–2 years in travel or returning to travel, <200 pax/yr, ₹1–2Cr turnover, building client base, need structured support.

From current pipeline: CNearMe Holidays, Trip Triangle, some newer South/Central India agents

  • Base commission tier (e.g. 60%)
  • Extended induction (3 days)
  • Weekly check-ins for first 3 months
  • Mandatory business plan within 30 days
  • 6-month performance review — move up or exit
Target: ₹50L gross/year · Monthly commitment: ₹2,000
EPP AGENCY

Tier 4 — Agency / Multi-Branch Partner

Equivalent to Travelosophers Agency Owner
Who: Established travel agencies with multiple branches, staff of 5+, turnover ₹10Cr+, wanting to white-label TF's product and back-office.

From current pipeline: Travco (Goa, 1000 pax, 10 staff, ₹30Cr), SGS Tour (Shimoga, 12,000 pax, 70 staff), RC GO (Goa), SOTC Allahabad, JK Tours (Pune)

  • Bespoke commercial agreement
  • Multi-staff training programme
  • Dedicated TF operations manager
  • Own brand option available
  • Volume-based commission negotiation
Target: ₹10Cr+ gross/year · Monthly: negotiated

Travelosophers "Why Us" → What TripFactory Can Claim & What It Needs to Build

12 reasons Travelosophers gives for joining — mapped against TF's current EPP position

Travelosophers publishes 12 headline reasons to join. Below is each one, with an honest assessment of whether TripFactory can already make the same claim, or whether it needs to build towards it.

🏆 50+ Years' Experience (Cyplon Travel, 1972)

Travelosophers trades on Cyplon Travel's 50-year heritage as a major trust signal for new PSAs.

✅ TF Can Match: TripFactory has 10+ years of operation and Vinay Gupta's industry track record. Market this more explicitly in EPP recruitment. "A decade of building India's travel industry."

💰 Leading Commission Terms

60–65% starting, scalable to 80%. Multiple commission approaches. Uncapped earnings. Positioned as market-leading in the UK homeworking sector.

⚠️ TF Needs to Build: No published commission terms currently. Once defined and published, TF can make a strong claim — Indian PSAs typically earn much less from traditional wholesale models.

🎓 Unrivalled Training Efficiency

"Our training is unrivalled in its efficiency — we want you to be earning as quickly as possible." Speed to first booking is the focus.

⚠️ TF Needs to Build: Structured induction with a "Days to First Booking" KPI would be a powerful differentiator. "EPP partners make their first booking within 2 weeks of joining."

🏗️ Multi-Million Pound Investment Backing

Significant investment behind Travelosophers with ambitious technology and growth roadmap.

✅ TF Can Match: TripFactory has institutional backing and a technology platform. Position this in EPP recruitment: "Backed by a technology-first travel company with 791 staff and a global infrastructure."

🏨 In-House Tour Operator & Contracting Team

"Our jewel in the crown — we can contract directly with suppliers to give you better margins." Dynamic packaging + direct hotel contracting = PSA earns more per booking.

✅ TF Can Match: TripFactory's direct supplier relationships and technology platform (TF Tech + hotel contracts) are exactly this. Make it a headline EPP benefit: "You access TF's direct contracts — better margins than any standalone agent."

🤝 Head Office Support + Dedicated BDM

Named BDM for every PSA. Day-to-day support. Quarterly business reviews. Admin team handles all after-sales centrally.

⚠️ TF Needs to Build: The field team exists but BDM roles aren't formalised per PSA. Naming an Account Manager for each EPP partner immediately closes this gap. Low cost, high impact.

🛡️ Financial Protection & Stability

ABTA + ATOL + IATA. "All bookings protected by leading industry bodies." This is a PSA's insurance policy against business failure.

⚠️ TF Needs to Build: TF has IATA but the full consumer protection story (what happens if TF closes? what covers the PSA's clients?) needs to be documented and communicated clearly to EPP partners.

📦 Dynamic Packaging

"We allow you to take control, build your own packages, one element at a time, and make bigger margins." This is positioned as a key income differentiator vs selling fixed tour operator packages.

✅ TF Can Match: TripFactory's tech platform already enables dynamic packaging. This should be a core EPP selling point — "Don't just sell someone else's package. Build your own. Keep the margin."

🌐 Part of Advantage Travel Partnership (£4.5bn collective)

Being part of the UK's largest independent travel group gives Travelosophers commercial leverage. PSAs benefit from the collective buying power.

⚠️ TF Needs to Build: TF could join TAAI (Travel Agents Association of India) or IATO as a collective and position EPP partners as members by extension. Gives instant credibility and supplier leverage.

✈️ Supplier Access

"Top suppliers — packages, hotels, cruises, flights, transfers and more." The breadth of supplier access is a major recruitment argument for PSAs who currently cobble together relationships independently.

✅ TF Can Match: TripFactory's supplier network is already strong. The key is communicating it. Create an "EPP Supplier Portfolio" document showing all available suppliers, destinations, and commission rates — a powerful recruitment and retention tool.

💻 Systems & Technology

"Constantly investing in technology with an exciting roadmap." Cloud-based, CRM-integrated, works anywhere. Positioned as a competitive advantage over standalone agents using spreadsheets.

✅ TF Can Match: TripFactory has a strong tech platform. Position this explicitly in EPP: "Stop using spreadsheets. TF gives you a professional booking platform, CRM, quotation system, and payment processing — from day one."

🏅 Award-Winning (Travel Weekly Homeworking Agency of the Year)

Industry recognition used to signal credibility and quality to prospective PSAs. "Best Small Homeworking Agency of the Year."

⚠️ TF Should Pursue: Enter TripFactory for TAAI / TAFI / Travel+Leisure India awards in the PSA/Franchise category. Even being shortlisted becomes a recruitment asset. Launch an internal "EPP Partner of the Year" award to generate content and community pride.
Summary — TF's "Why Join EPP" Scorecard:

✅ Can claim today (6/12): Industry experience, in-house contracting, dynamic packaging, technology platform, supplier access, back-office support.

⚠️ Need to build (6/12): Published commission terms, structured training, named BDM per PSA, formal consumer protection story, industry body membership, award/recognition programme.
